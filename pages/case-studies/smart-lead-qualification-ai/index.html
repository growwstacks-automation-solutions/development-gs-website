200+ Applications a Day, and Every One Needed Human Eyes

The client ‚Äî an e-commerce brand running community ambassador and influencer programs ‚Äî was receiving over 200 applications daily through Instagram DMs and Facebook Messenger. Each one needed to be read, evaluated against qualification criteria (location, audience size, content niche, engagement quality), and either approved, rejected, or flagged for follow-up.

Their marketing team was spending 2-3 hours every morning just doing initial screening. Qualification standards drifted depending on who was reviewing. Response times stretched to 24-48 hours, and by then, 35% of interested prospects had already moved on ‚Äî either losing interest or signing up with a competitor's program. The tools were disconnected too: ManyChat captured the conversations, but someone had to manually copy the relevant data into Slack for the team and then compose individual Gmail follow-ups with program materials. It was slow, inconsistent, and expensive.

An AI Agent That Reads, Evaluates, and Routes ‚Äî In Seconds

We built an intelligent qualification pipeline that connects ManyChat's conversation capture directly to ChatGPT-4 for evaluation, with Make.com orchestrating the entire workflow. The moment a prospect completes a conversation flow on Instagram or Messenger, the system evaluates their responses against the brand's specific qualification criteria, assigns a score, and takes action ‚Äî all without a human touching it.

End-to-end system architecture: ManyChat ‚Üí ChatGPT-4 ‚Üí Make.com ‚Üí Slack + Gmail

Qualified prospects automatically receive detailed program materials via Gmail (with attachments), the team gets a rich Slack notification with the prospect's profile and qualification summary, and the ManyChat thread updates so the prospect knows their application is being processed. Unqualified prospects get a polite decline message. The entire cycle takes seconds, not days.

How the Qualification Pipeline Works

The system operates as a four-stage pipeline, each stage handing off to the next automatically:

  1. Conversation Capture (ManyChat): Structured conversation flows collect qualification data ‚Äî location, audience size, content focus, engagement metrics, and interest level. When the conversation completes, ManyChat fires a webhook to Make.com with all collected fields.
  2. AI Evaluation (ChatGPT-4): Make.com sends the full conversation context to ChatGPT-4 along with the brand's qualification criteria. The AI evaluates intent, fit, and readiness, assigns a qualification score, and returns a structured decision: qualified, not qualified, or needs more information.
  3. Conditional Routing (Make.com): Based on the AI's decision, Make.com routes the prospect through different paths. Qualified leads trigger both a Slack notification and a Gmail sequence. Incomplete conversations get a follow-up prompt back through ManyChat.
  4. Multi-Channel Delivery: Slack receives a formatted notification with the prospect's profile, qualification score, conversation history, and recommended next steps. Gmail sends program materials with attachments. ManyChat updates the prospect's thread with a status confirmation.
The Make.com scenario handling webhook ingestion, AI evaluation, conditional routing, and multi-channel delivery

The ChatGPT-4 evaluation prompt was the key engineering challenge. We designed it to not just check boxes, but to understand conversational nuance ‚Äî detecting genuine enthusiasm vs. casual browsing, identifying prospects who match the brand aesthetic even if their follower count is modest, and flagging incomplete responses for follow-up rather than outright rejection. This single prompt took 3 iterations to get the qualification accuracy to 92%.

Inside the Technical Architecture

The Make.com scenario is the nervous system of this build. It receives ManyChat webhooks containing structured conversation data (custom fields mapped for each qualification attribute), passes the full payload to the ChatGPT-4 API with a carefully engineered system prompt, parses the JSON response for qualification decisions and scores, then fans out to multiple downstream actions based on conditional logic.

ManyChat conversation flow with structured qualification questions and webhook data transmission

We chose Make.com over Zapier for this build specifically because of the conditional routing requirements. The scenario needed to evaluate multiple conditions simultaneously ‚Äî qualification score thresholds, response completeness flags, geography filters ‚Äî and route to different notification combinations. Make.com's visual branching made this straightforward; in Zapier, this would have required multiple separate Zaps with fragmented logic.

The ChatGPT-4 integration uses a structured JSON response format with explicit fields for decision (qualified/not_qualified/needs_info), confidence_score (0-100), key_strengths (array), concerns (array), and recommended_action. This structured output made downstream routing deterministic ‚Äî no ambiguous free-text parsing.

The key is structured prompting: we define explicit qualification criteria, require JSON-formatted responses with confidence scores, and design the AI to flag uncertain cases for human review rather than making binary decisions on borderline prospects. Every project includes 30 days of free support post-launch to fine-tune qualification thresholds based on actual lead quality feedback.

The system is designed to err on the side of inclusion, not exclusion. Instead of binary qualified/rejected decisions, the AI returns three outcomes: qualified, not qualified, and needs more information. Borderline cases always get routed to the "needs more info" path, triggering a follow-up conversation rather than a rejection.

Additionally, every AI decision includes a confidence score (0-100). Decisions below an 80% confidence threshold are automatically flagged for human review in Slack, with the AI's reasoning attached so a team member can make the final call in seconds.

This safety net means false rejections are near-zero ‚Äî the system only auto-rejects prospects where the AI is 90%+ confident they don't meet criteria.
